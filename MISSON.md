# Mission and Hope

*A companion to the profile README, which describes method. This describes purpose.*

---

## The Mission

Build AI systems that ground their claims in verifiable observation instead of confident pattern-matching — because ungrounded confidence, deployed at scale, is dangerous.

Prove the method works on small, falsifiable questions before trusting it on large ones.

That is the whole of it. Not a claim about what this will become — a statement of what is being attempted, right now, with the tools at hand.

---

## The Hope

If terrain-grounded, sequentially-constrained observation really does produce something block-model reasoning structurally cannot — a verifiable record instead of hindsight-informed confabulation — that principle might generalize. Not just to video. To bigger questions. More consequential ones. Query optimization. Decision-making under uncertainty. The gap between what an AI system confidently says and what it can actually stand behind.

This is held as a hope, not a premise. It is not assumed true in order to justify the work — it is the thing the work might, if it holds up, earn the right to claim.

---

## Why the Two Are Kept Separate

Provenance over polished mythology: an aspiration and a finding are different kinds of statement, and collapsing them is exactly the failure mode this project is built to guard against.

**Good outweighs the bad — but only once it's been shown to, not before.**

The mission is what's being attempted. The hope is what it might mean if it works. EXP7 is the test of whether it does.
